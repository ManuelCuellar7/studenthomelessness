Student Homelessness in New York has duplicated in the last decade.

By Manuel Cuellar

November 18th, 2021

Approximately 70 percent of homeless New Yorkers in the city’s shelters are families with children Credit: WIN family shelter

There's been a 42% increase in student homelessness since the start of the decade. Each year, approximately 30,000 students in New York spend time living in shelters. At least 3,860 were unsheltered, living in cars, parks, or abandoned buildings.

While the total number of students reported to be living in temporary housing was 9% lower last year than in 2019–20, the pandemic likely impeded schools’ ability to identify students experiencing homelessness. Many students learned remotely for the entirety of the 2020–21 school year, making it less likely that school staff would become aware of changes to their housing situations. In addition, overall school enrollment also fell last year, by 3.3%, and grade-by-grade, declines in homelessness followed trends in overall enrollment; in both cases, the most significant drops occurred in pre-K and kindergarten.
